<p><p>The gun metal market is primarily categorized into two types: copper-based alloys and lead-based alloys. Copper-based alloys, which typically include copper, tin, and zinc, are known for their corrosion resistance, durability, and excellent machinability, making them ideal for various applications. Lead-based alloys, on the other hand, incorporate lead for enhanced wear resistance and machinability, often finding use in applications requiring high precision, such as gears and valves. Both types serve distinct industries and demand varying manufacturing processes.</p></p>
