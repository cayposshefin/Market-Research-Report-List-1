<p><p>The box making films market comprises various types, each with distinct properties. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) films are known for their durability and versatility, often used for packaging. LDPE (low-density polyethylene) films are flexible and resistant to moisture, making them ideal for protective packaging. HDPE (high-density polyethylene) films are strong and rigid, suitable for heavier items. PP (polypropylene) films are lightweight yet robust, commonly used for food packaging. PET (polyethylene terephthalate) films offer excellent clarity and strength, making them popular for both packaging and display.</p></p>
