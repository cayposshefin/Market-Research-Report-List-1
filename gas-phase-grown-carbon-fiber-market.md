<p><ul><li>PAN Based</li><li>Asphalt Based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The gas-phase-grown carbon fiber market comprises primarily two types: PAN-based and asphalt-based fibers. PAN-based carbon fibers are derived from polyacrylonitrile, offering superior mechanical properties and thermal stability, making them ideal for aerospace and automotive applications. Conversely, asphalt-based carbon fibers are produced from petroleum bitumen, typically resulting in lower mechanical strength but enhanced electrical conductivity, suitable for applications in electronics and energy storage. Together, these types cater to diverse industrial needs, emphasizing performance and material properties.</p></p>
